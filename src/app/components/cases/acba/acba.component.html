ACBA-CREDIT AGRICOLE BANK is one of the largest banks in Armenia. And like most big brands in the country, every year it designs and distributes paper calendars for its clients and partners.

Branded calendars have become a deeply ingrained habit, just like the much more dangerous of using plastic bags – Armenians are so used to them that a population of less than 3 million people uses over 3000 tons of plastic bags every year.

Our goal was to stand up against the environmental disaster caused by plastic bags with a campaign that would not only raise awareness, but actually help people give up the habit of using plastic bags.

CalenDARE Against Plastic

Composed of four bags made with recycled paper and fit to hold lightweight items, the CalenDare dares everyone to spend 2020 with as little plastic as possible.

Handing out 12000 calendars from the bank’s 60 locations, we got our message into thousands of homes and workplaces, and turned ordinary shoppers into walking protests, all with zero spending on media placement.

But, paper bags are not enough – we need to switch to better alternatives. To get our message to more people and show them what’s really going on, we launched a video campaign on social media and took the CalenDare outdoors.

outcome

Though plastic pollution isn’t usually a hot topic in Armenia, the Calendare quickly started long-overdue discussions on the issue.

This was the first calendar that people didn’t just politely accept, but actively asked for.

Public TV of Armenia, NGOs, other brands, influencers and state officials, including Armenia’s Minister of Environment, joined the campaign.

But the most important result took place in people’s minds. With more and more people accepting our dare, we made a real step towards a plastic-free future.
